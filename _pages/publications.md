## Working Papers

### Targeted Electricity Subsidies and Rural Development: Evidence from India's RGGVY Program

This paper studies whether targeted electricity subsidies can improve rural welfare outcomes in India. It evaluates the Rajiv Gandhi Grameen Vidyutikaran Yojana (RGGVY), which provided free electricity connections to Below Poverty Line households. Using IHDS panel data for 2004-05 and 2011-12, together with district-level variation in programme rollout, the paper applies a Difference-in-Differences design based on years of programme exposure.

The analysis finds that RGGVY increased electricity connections and ownership of basic electric appliances among BPL households. It also suggests gains in income, consumption expenditure, non-agricultural income, non-farm work, and basic educational outcomes, with stronger benefits among targeted households.

**Presented at:** 20th Annual Conference on Economic Growth and Development, Indian Statistical Institute, New Delhi, December 2025.

**Accepted for presentation at:** 5th Asian Economic Development Conference, Hong Kong; Nordic Conference in Development Economics, Sweden; Royal Economic Society Annual Conference 2026, Newcastle University, UK.

### Rural Electrification and the Demographic Transition in India

This paper estimates the long-run impact of rural electrification on fertility and maternal health behaviour using NFHS-4 and NFHS-5 microdata, covering approximately 1.4 million observations. It applies Two-Way Fixed Effects, Instrumental Variables, and Difference-in-Differences approaches based on the rollout of RGGVY.

The study examines whether electrification reduces fertility and birth incidence through increased media exposure, improved maternal healthcare access, institutional deliveries, and lower desired family size.

**Presented at:** 7th Annual Economics Conference, Ahmedabad University, Ahmedabad, January 2026.

### Does Digital Identification Improve Disability Welfare Delivery? Evidence from the Integration of the Unique Disability ID System in India

This paper evaluates whether digital identification improves welfare delivery for persons with disabilities. It exploits the staggered rollout of the Unique Disability ID programme using state-district panel data for 2014-2023 and applies a Difference-in-Differences design.

The findings suggest an increase in service intensity and improved targeting in districts where the programme was implemented early.

**Presented at:** International Conference on Digitalisation, Market and Society, IISER Mohali, December 2025.

## Academic Projects

### Lives in Transition: Exploring Livelihoods and Service Access in Aarey Colony

**Guide:** Prof. Srijit Mishra

This project examined the relationship between infrastructure access and capability deprivation in Aarey Colony, Mumbai. Drawing on the capability approach of Amartya Sen and Martha Nussbaum, it studied access to water, electricity, and basic services among tribal and migrant communities. The project involved field research through 25 in-depth interviews and provided evidence on urban inequality, health vulnerability, and service-delivery challenges.

### Female Workforce Trends in India: Insights from PLFS Data

**Guide:** Prof. S. Chandrashekhar

This project used Periodic Labour Force Survey data from 2017 to 2022 to study female workforce trends in India. The work involved data organization, merging, variable transformation, and descriptive analysis of large-scale household labour-force data.
